# Style Guide

## Target Reader

We write for people who want to act on the brokenness of the world — **regardless of faith background**. Not a Christian on-ramp, not a secular-vs-religious argument. Someone who's already convinced something is wrong and wants to know what to do about it, or someone who needs to feel the weight of the problem before they'll care about a solution.

**Assume no prior familiarity with development economics, international relations, or policy jargon.** A reader who doesn't know what "Optimum Currency Area theory" means until we explain it is the default reader, not the exception.

## Voice

Same discipline as *Christianity Reconstructed*: be direct, be affirmative, respect the reader, build toward the roadmap rather than scoring points against people who disagree.

**The specific failure mode to avoid, named explicitly by Marty during the original discussion:** dense and clinical. More pages is not the same as more urgent. A reader who feels lectured at by statistics will put the book down before reaching the roadmap that's the actual point.

## Two jobs, never confuse them

**Urgency sections — light curation, not original investigation.**
The value is finding and retelling the most viscerally compelling *existing* human stories and documented cases — specific journalism, specific people, specific places. Citing a report clinically ("733 million people are undernourished") does not create urgency. Retelling what that number means for one real, named situation does.

**Blueprint sections — heavy synthesis, not invention.**
Do not originate new economic or political mechanisms from scratch — that's hubris, and it won't survive scrutiny from anyone who actually knows the field. The value we add is assembling five separate, already-serious bodies of research (open-borders economics, monetary union theory, Housing First, education-equity research, UN roadmap work) into one coherent, sequenced blueprint nobody has combined before. This mirrors exactly how *Christianity Reconstructed*'s real value-add was the "humanity growing up" reframing and the Epicurus response — not re-deriving scripture from scratch.

## Citation Discipline

Same rigor as *Christianity Reconstructed*'s ESV references — real sourcing and attribution throughout, both for honesty and because a skeptical secular reader will fact-check harder than a faith-sympathetic one. Every empirical claim in a blueprint section traces to a real, named source (see `research/victory-conditions.md`). Never round a "some economists estimate" up to a fact.

## Honesty About Gaps

Where the existing research is thin (currency, education — see `research/victory-conditions.md`), say so directly instead of papering over it with confident-sounding prose. A reader who catches an overclaim on one condition will distrust all five.

## No LLM Idioms

Avoid stock phrasing that reads as AI-generated rather than authored: "worth sitting with," "it's worth noting," and similar hedge-and-pause constructions. Flagged directly by Marty (2026-07-13) on PR #3. Say the thing directly instead of gesturing at how the reader should feel about it.
